Proposed 33-story building could become Evanston's tallest construction

by Xin Liu Oct.16, 2017

The Evanston Preservation Commission voted on Tuesday to recommend the construction plan of what could become the tallest building in the city.

The 33-story residential building would stand at the corner of Davis Street and Chicago Avenue beside a 2-story landmarked building. The site is currently occupied by a parking lot and a Chase Bank drive-thru, according to the construction plan by Vermilion Enterprises, LLC, a Chicago real estate company.

“It’s definitely an underutilized site and especially a missing tooth along Davis Street … and this project basically fills that in,” said Kerry Dickson, the director of residential real estate at Vermilion.

Martha Koch, the owner of the landmarked building, said she had partnered with Vermilion to make sure the plan was designed with the preservation of the landmarked building in mind.

Many attendees expressed their concerns through raising questions about the project.

Carl Klein, a recent graduate in historic preservation, said sealing the 6-foot gap between the two buildings isn’t enough to protect the landmarked building from rain and would also make future restoration constructions harder.

A few attendees were less critical of the project and made positive comments.

Dale Bradley, 47, said he would move to Evanston soon and was glad the construction of the building could bring more people and jobs to the neighborhood.

“I think it's nothing but an improvement,” Bradley said.

The plan will be proposed to the Evanston Plan Commission for another review before it goes to the Evanston City Council for a vote.
